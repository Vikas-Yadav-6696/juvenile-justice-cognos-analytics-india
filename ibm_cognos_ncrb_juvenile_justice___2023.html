Key findings & insights

Maharashtra Backlog Crisis

Mumbai (backlog ratio 5.08) and Pune (5.33) carry caseloads 5× larger than annual apprehensions — a structural judicial capacity failure requiring fast-track courts.

Best-Practice Cities

Ahmedabad, Kochi (98.2%), Surat (100%) and Kolkata (100%) achieved complete or near-complete annual resolution — demonstrating achievable benchmarks for all cities.

Rehabilitation Dominance

46.1% of resolved cases used non-punitive routes, aligning with JJ Act 2015. Indore is an outlier with a 30% conviction rate and 5.84% severity index.

Delhi — Volume Leader

Delhi accounts for 41.2% of national apprehensions (3,098 of 7,521) with a 67.2% resolution rate and 94.6% guilty verdict — the most resource-intensive jurisdiction.

Inactive Reporting Cities

Ghaziabad reported zero activity. Lucknow and Kanpur carry backlogs of 37 and 151 cases with no new apprehensions — data quality review warranted.

Imprisonment Rate Very Low

Only 145 of 16,200 juveniles (0.89%) were awarded imprisonment nationally — largely consistent with JJ Act intent. Chennai and Indore exceed 3%.

1 Resolution Rate %

= (Total Cases − Pending End) ÷ Total Cases × 100

Measures the share of all cases closed during 2023. A rate below 50% means the system is accumulating caseload faster than clearing it. National figure: 43.9%.

2 Rehabilitation Rate %

= (Sent Home + Sent to Special Home) ÷ Total × 100

Captures non-punitive, restorative disposals. Core to JJ Act 2015 philosophy. Best cities (Kochi, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad) exceed 94%.

3 Conviction Rate %

= (Fined + Imprisoned) ÷ Total Cases × 100

Tracks punitive outcomes. Ideally kept as low as possible. Indore (30.04%) and Chennai (5.27%) are outliers requiring urgent review.

4 Backlog Ratio

= Pending at Beginning ÷ Apprehended During Year

Ratio >1 signals that inherited backlog exceeds new-case volume — systemic stress. Mumbai (5.08) and Pune (5.33) are at critical levels.

5 Severity Index %

= Juveniles Awarded Imprisonment ÷ Total Cases × 100

Key human-rights indicator. Cities above 3% warrant judicial review. Nationally only 0.89% — largely compliant with the JJ Act spirit.
